Since I cannot read the image directly, I will use the standard definitions for these biomarker types as established by the FDA-NIH Biomarker Working Group (BEST):
1. **Pharmacodynamic biomarker (PDY)**: Used to show that a biological response has occurred in a person who has been exposed to a medical product or an environmental agent.
2. **Safety biomarker (SAF)**: Measured to indicate the likelihood, presence, or extent of toxicity as an adverse effect.
3. **Monitoring biomarker (MOI)**: Measured serially to assess status of a disease or medical condition or for evidence of exposure to (or effects of) a medical product or environmental agent.
4. **Predictive biomarker (PRD)**: Used to identify individuals who are more likely than similar individuals without the biomarker to experience a favorable or unfavorable effect from exposure to a medical product or an environmental agent.
5. **Surrogate endpoint (SUR)**: An endpoint used in clinical trials as a substitute for a direct measure of how a patient feels, functions, or survives.
